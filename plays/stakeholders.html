Communicate and engage with your stakeholders

Build a communications plan for special projects or business-as-usual.

Use this play to...

  • Optimize how you share information with stakeholders
  • Build a communications plan for a specific project or for your team’s regular operations
  • Build strong relationships with your key stakeholders and partners.

Running the play

Be intentional about how and when information gets communicated. Use this play when your team is forming and establishing operating norms, you’re embarking on a change management process, or past communication has not gone as planned.

Prepare for the play

Use the template - or create a table with similar headers - in preparation for the play.

Prior to beginning the play, ensure everyone comes to the exercise with a brainstormed list of your stakeholders.

If available, the meeting facilitator should come to the meeting with a list of the team’s existing communications with stakeholders, including what and why you’re communicating, and the channel(s) you typically use.

Define your goal

Create a statement, or group of statements, describing what you are aiming to achieve in preparing a stakeholder communication plan. Keep it short. Have one person scribe while the whole group brainstorms. Set a fixed period for this part of the play. Your statements don’t have to be perfect at this stage. You can always wordsmith it later if need be.

Some examples of goals for a communication plan may be:

  • Build trust with our stakeholders
  • Reduce duplicated efforts and dependency bottlenecks
  • Educate other teams about what we’re doing and how it may affect their work

Define your stakeholders

Break out into small groups and review the stakeholder brainstorms everyone prepared as pre-work. Agree on the key stakeholders to be included in your communications plan. For each stakeholder, define the relationship to your project/team. Are they a decision maker? Client? Subject matter expert? Employee? Initiative partner?

Present back to the wider team and work to gain consensus on the stakeholders for whom you are building.

Define your communication methods

Determine the most effective way to communicate with each stakeholder.

Think about what content you’ll be sharing as well as the tool you’ll use to share it. For example, with initiative partner leads, you might choose to communicate through an online platform dedicated to sharing minor updates and asking for input from each other.

Create your communication plan

Use the table you created as pre-work to start building out a communications plan. For each frequency, write down the audience you’ll communicate with, the channels you’ll use, what you’ll communicate, and links to further details or artifacts. After you’ve created your communications plan, agree on an owner (you may even assign an owner to specific communications).
