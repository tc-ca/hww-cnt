Use the Sustainable Transportation Assessment Tool (STAT)

Do you write any of the following: Memoranda to Cabinet, Treasury Board submissions, regulatory amendments or any other material requiring Minister or Cabinet approval? If yes, you must use the Sustainable Transportation Assessment Tool (STAT).

Use this play to...

  • Assess potential economic, social, and environmental effects early in policy development
  • Help management make informed and inclusive policy decisions

Running the play

The Sustainable Transportation Assessment Tool (STAT) is a planning tool that allows a qualitative assessment of potential economic, social and environmental effects early in the policy and program development process. The STAT includes questions as a means to assess the potential risks of your initiative and is integrated into the Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA) process. 

By using this tool early in the process of developing a proposal for a policy or program, you can determine the likelihood of positive outcomes or adverse effects as a result of implementing a policy, plan, or program proposal and in turn, prepare for them.  

The STAT’s benefits include:  

  • Interactive  
  • Easy to use  
  • Helps management make more informed and coordinated decisions  
  • Prompts you to think outside silos  
  • Allows you to save time and extra work in the end

Other resources on this topic

  • Departments and agencies are expected to consider potential environmental effects when developing policies, plans and program proposals: Cabinet Directive on the Environmental Assessment of Policy, Plan and Program Proposals (Canada, 2016) 
  • Transport Canada’s vision of a sustainable transportation system is one that integrates and balances social, economic and environmental objectives: Policy Statement: Strategic Environmental Assessment at Transport Canada (RDIMS: 7936625) (Transport Canada, 2013) (accessible only on the Transport Canada network)
